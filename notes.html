Inversion Methods

Inversion techniques are used to interpret geophysical data, especially in seismic and gravity surveys. Check out the notes below:

Introduction to Inversion

Menke's work in inversion theory is fundamental to understanding how to approach inverse problems in geophysics. His book explains the mathematical foundations of linear and nonlinear inversion methods, emphasizing how to apply these techniques to geophysical data. Menke provides a rigorous mathematical framework for solving inversion problems, helping researchers understand how to model data and extract meaningful parameters from it, especially in the presence of noise and model uncertainty..

Geophysical Inversion Techniques

Richardson’s contributions to inversion methods focus on refining and improving computational techniques used to solve inverse problems. His work often addresses the practical challenges in applying inversion in real-world geophysical research, such as improving the resolution and robustness of models. Richardson’s methods provide insights into overcoming the limitations posed by noise in data and ensuring the inversion process yields reliable results for complex geophysical systems..
